FVD vs FVE 📌 Most Important
FVD — Hypovolemia
Causes
Hemorrhage · Vomiting · Diarrhea · Burns · Diuretics · Fever · Impaired thirst
Signs — Mild
Orthostatic hypotension · Increased HR · Restlessness · Weight loss
Signs — Moderate
Confusion · Extreme thirst · Nausea · Cool clammy skin · Rapid pulse · Urine <30ml/hr
Signs — Severe
↓ Cardiac output · Unconsciousness · Hypotension · Absent pulses
Treatment
Find cause → Replace fluids orally or IV · Use D5W (hypotonic)
FVE — Hypervolemia
Causes
Heart failure · Early renal failure · Excess IV therapy · High sodium intake · Corticosteroids
Signs
Tachypnea · Dyspnea · Crackles (lungs) · Bounding pulse · Hypertension · Distended neck veins · Acute weight gain · Edema · Orthopnea
Treatment
Find cause · Fluid & sodium restriction · Diuretics (if not renal failure)
🐟 بصموجة عمو غيث — FVD vs FVE
DDry mouth · Decreased urine · Dizzy = FVD
EEdema · Engorged veins · Extra weight = FVE

Potassium (K⁺) Normal: 3.5–5 mEq/L
Hyperkalemia ↑
Causes
High K⁺ intake · Excess salt substitutes · Rapid K⁺ IV infusion
Signs
Arrhythmias · ↓ cardiac contraction · Cardiac arrest · N/V/D · Muscle weakness · Abdominal distention
Nursing — Key Drugs
Calcium gluconate → ↓ myocardial irritability
Insulin + IV glucose → moves K⁺ back into cells
Kayexalate → exchanges Na⁺ for K⁺ in intestine
Hypokalemia ↓
Causes
GI losses (diarrhea, vomiting, NGT suction) · Laxatives · Prolonged gastric suctioning
Signs
Fatigue · Muscle weakness · Cardiac arrest · Orthostatic hypotension · Respiratory weakness · ↑ glucose
Nursing
K⁺ replacement as prescribed · High-K foods: bananas · orange juice · dried fruit · Emergency equipment ready
🐟 بصموجة عمو غيث — Potassium
HIGH KCAN kill: Cardiac arrest · Arrhythmia · Nausea
→ Give Calcium gluconate first!
LOW KBowels flush K out → Bananas bring K back
→ Weak, tired, arrhythmia

Physical Assessment Quick Reference 📌 Know These
Signs → FVD
Headache · Dizziness · Sunken eyes · Dry lips & mucous membranes · Flat neck veins · ↑ Pulse · ↓ Cap refill · Oliguria/Anuria · Inelastic skin turgor · Cold clammy skin · Sunken abdomen
Signs → FVE
Periorbital edema · Distended neck veins · Edema (legs, sacrum, back) · Bounding pulse · Hypertension · Crackles · Dyspnea · ↑ RR · Distended abdomen · Diuresis
Laboratory Tests 📌 Key Values
Must-Know Labs
🔹 Serum electrolytes — determines hydration status (blood plasma)
🔹 CBC — number & type of RBCs and WBCs per mm³
🔹 Blood creatinine — kidney function indicator
🔹 BUN : Creatinine ratio — normal = 10 : 1
🔹 ABG (Arterial Blood Gas) — acid-base balance status
🔹 Urine specific gravity — urine concentration
Intake & Output (I&O) Monitoring
Intake
PO fluids · Ice cream/gelatin · Tube feeding · Hyperalimentation · IV Primary · IV Piggyback · Blood/blood products
Output
Urine · Emesis (vomit) · GI suction · Drainage · Chest tube · Diarrhea
💡 Record every 24 hrs. Foley catheter: record each shift or more. Ambulatory patients self-record each bathroom trip.
