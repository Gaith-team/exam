🫀 Body System Assessment ⭐ EXAM

Respiratory
Stop smoking 4–8 weeks before surgery. Postpone if respiratory infection present.
Cardiovascular
Postpone if uncontrolled hypertension.
Hepatic/Renal
Acute liver disease = high surgical mortality. Affects anesthetic metabolism.
Endocrine (Diabetes)
Risk of hypoglycemia during anesthesia & hyperglycemia → wound infection.
Immune
Identify & document all allergies and medication sensitivities.
Drug/Alcohol
Postpone surgery if acutely intoxicated (susceptible to injury).

🩹 Pre-op Preparation ⭐ EXAM

  • Informed consent — must be signed
  • Teaching — deep breathing, coughing, leg exercises
  • Skin prep — antibacterial soap, shave immediately before OR
  • Bowel prep — laxatives/enemas if needed
  • Urinary catheter if required (especially pyloric surgery)
  • NPO — nothing by mouth 8–12 hours before general anesthesia
  • Remove: jewelry, artificial nails, dentures, lenses

💊 Pre-medication Purposes ⭐ EXAM

  • Anxiety/Pain Benzodiazepines
  • Amnesia Lorazepam, Midazolam
  • ↓ Secretions Atropine / Hyoscine
  • ↓ Gastric acid Ranitidine (Zantac)
  • ↓ PONV Antihistamines, Butyrophenones
  • ↑ Hypnosis Morphine, Pethidine
  • Given 30–60 min before OR

⚠️ Intraoperative Complications ⭐ EXAM

Nausea/Vomiting Anaphylaxis Hypoxia Hypothermia Malignant Hyperthermia DIC

💉 Types of Anesthesia ⭐ EXAM

1. General
Pain controlled by general insensibility
Agents: Halothane, Nitrous oxide, Methohexital sodium (IV)
2. Local/Regional Block
Sensory nerves in one area anesthetized
3. Spinal/Epidural
Blocked below diaphragm — no loss of consciousness
🟣 Phase III — Post-operative

📋 PACU Assessment (Immediate) ⭐ EXAM

  • A — Airway: artificial airway presence
  • B — Breathing / Vital signs + pulse oximeter
  • C — Consciousness: follows commands, moves extremities
  • D — Dressing / suture line
  • E — oxygenation: skin color, nail beds, capillary refill
  • F — Fluids: IV rate, amount in bottle
  • P — Position of patient

⏱ Vital Signs Monitoring Schedule ⭐ EXAM

😣 Common Post-op Discomforts ⭐ EXAM

Nausea/Vomiting Restlessness Thirst Constipation Abdominal distention Pain Urinary retention Hiccups

🎯 Post-op Goals (Expected Outcomes)

  • Optimal respiratory function
  • Relief of pain & discomforts
  • Freedom from injury
  • Adequate tissue perfusion & fluid volume
  • Nutritional balance
  • Normal urinary & bowel function
  • Skin integrity & no infection
  • Return of mobility
  • Reduction of anxiety & psychological well-being

⭐ Quick Exam Reference

📌 Must-Know Numbers & Facts

NPO before general anesthesia: 8–12 hours
Stop smoking before surgery: 4–8 weeks
Pre-medication given: 30–60 min before OR
Urgent surgery: Within 24–30 hours
VS frequency (stable): q15min → q30min → q4h
Avoid heavy lifting after major surgery: ≥6 weeks
Bowel movement check: If none after 2–3 days post-peristalsis → act
Position changes (bedridden): Every 2 hours
