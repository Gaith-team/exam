📚 Definition

🫁 What is Pneumonia? ⭐ EXAM

Inflammation of the lung parenchyma — organisms inhaled → transmitted to lower airways & alveoli → impairs gas exchange. Air sacs fill with pus and may become solid.
Double Pneumonia
Both lungs affected
Single Pneumonia
One lung affected
Lobar Pneumonia
Only certain lobes
🦠 Classification by Causative Agent ⭐ EXAM

🔵 Bacterial — Gram-Positive ⭐ EXAM

  • Pneumococcal
  • Staphylococcal
  • Streptococcal
  • Anthrax — also called wool-sorter's disease
    • Disease of sheep/cattle → humans via inhalation
    • Agricultural/industrial exposure to contaminated animal hides or hair
    • Lethal if not treated before severe symptoms

🔴 Bacterial — Gram-Negative ⭐ EXAM

  • Influenza (Haemophilus)
  • Pseudomonas
  • Klebsiella
  • Legionnaires
Other types:
Mycoplasma Viral — most common Protozoa Pneumocystis carinii — in AIDS/Chemo patients Fungi, Parasites, Rickettsiae, Chlamydiae

🌡️ Clinical Manifestations (Bacterial) ⭐ EXAM

🔥 Signs & Symptoms

  • Sudden onset + shaking chill
  • Rapidly rising fever: 39.5°–40.5°C (101°–105°F)
  • Cough productive of purulent sputum (pus)
  • Pleuritic chest pain — aggravated by breathing/coughing
  • Tachypnea + respiratory grunting + nasal flaring + accessory muscles
  • Tachycardia
  • Rapid, bounding pulse
  • Fatigue, poor appetite

⚠️ Complications ⭐ EXAM

  • Pleural effusion
  • Hypotension & Shock especially gram−, elderly
  • Superinfection pericarditis, bacteremia, meningitis
  • Delirium ⚡ Medical emergency!
  • Atelectasis due to mucous plugs
  • Delayed resolution
📝 Elderly Special Note:
Assess for: unusual behavior, altered mental status, dehydration, excessive fatigue, concomitant heart failure

🩺 Nursing Process (I–V) ⭐ EXAM

I. Assessment ⭐ EXAM

  • Changes in temperature & pulse
  • Amount, odor, color of secretions
  • Frequency & severity of cough
  • Degree of tachypnea / SOB
  • Physical assessment: inspect & auscultate chest
  • Changes in chest X-ray

III. Goals / Planning ⭐ EXAM

  • Improve airway patency
  • Rest to conserve energy
  • Maintain fluid balance
  • Maintain adequate nutrition
  • Patient education & preventive measures
  • Prevention of complications

IV. Nursing Interventions ⭐ EXAM

  • Airway: auscultate lungs, monitor ABGs/pulse oximetry
  • Reduce secretions: breathing exercises, chest physiotherapy (percussion, vibration, postural drainage), fluid intake
  • Rest: bed rest + position changes, sedatives if needed
  • Fluids: ↑ intake to 2 L/day if not contraindicated; I&O chart
  • Education: breathing exercises, smoking cessation, influenza vaccine

⭐ Quick Exam Reference

📌 Must-Know Facts

🌡️
Fever range: 39.5°–40.5°C (101°–105°F)
💧
Fluid intake goal: 2 Liters/day
⚡
Delirium = medical emergency
🦠
Most common type: Viral pneumonia
🐑
Anthrax = wool-sorter's disease — lethal if untreated
🧪
Protozoa (Pneumocystis carinii) → AIDS / Chemo patients
🚬
Smoking destroys tracheobronchial ciliary action = 1st line of lung defense
💉
Influenza vaccine → prevents secondary bacterial infections (Staph, H. influenza, S. pneumonia)
