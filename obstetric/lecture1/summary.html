üå∏ External Female Genitalia (Vulva)

6 Components: "MLLCVP"

1. Mons Pubis (Mons Veneris)

Location: Over symphysis pubis

Structure: Pad of fat, covered with pubic hair (after puberty)

Function: Protection of symphysis during intercourse

2. Labia Majora (Large Lips)

Location: Arise in mons veneris, merge into perineum

Structure: 2 folds of fat/areolar tissue, skin + pubic hair, sweat & sebaceous glands

Function: Protection of vaginal introitus

3. Labia Minora (Small Lips)

Location: Between labia majora

Structure: 2 thin hairless folds, anteriorly enclose clitoris, posteriorly form fourchette

Function: Lubricate vulva, swell with stimulation, highly sensitive

4. Clitoris

Location: Anterior junction of labia minora

Structure: Small erectile organ, very sensitive, highly vascular (like male penis)

Function: Sexual stimulation, orgasm

5. Vestibule

Location: Region between labia minora

Contains:

‚Ä¢ Urethral Orifice: 2.5 cm posterior to clitoris + Skene's glands (mucus secretion)
‚Ä¢ Vaginal Orifice (Introitus): Posterior 2/3 of vestibule, partially closed by hymen
‚Ä¢ Hymen: Thin membrane (intact in virgins), tears during intercourse/childbirth
‚Ä¢ Bartholin's Glands: 2 small glands, open either side of vaginal orifice, mucus for lubrication during intercourse

6. Perineal Body

Location: Between vulva and anus (most posterior part)

Structure: Skin, muscle, fascia

Function: Strengthens pelvic floor

Clinical: Can lacerate/be incised (episiotomy) during childbirth

üè• Internal Female Genitalia (4)

O-F-U-V

1. Ovaries (Female Gonads)

Location: Either side of uterus, below/behind fimbriated ends

Size: Like almonds - 4cm long √ó 2cm wide √ó 1cm thick, Weight 2-5gm

Structure: Medulla (fibrous, blood vessels) + Cortex (ovarian follicles)

Functions: 1) Develop & release ovum, 2) Secrete hormones (estrogen, progesterone, androgen)

Support: Ovarian ligament (medial), Infundibulopelvic ligament (lateral)

2. Fallopian Tubes (Uterine Tubes)

Length: 10 cm total

Location: Extend from uterine cornua ‚Üí sidewalls of pelvis, arch over ovaries

4 Parts (I-I-A-I):

1. Interstitial (1cm): Within uterine wall, Lumen 1mm
2. Isthmus (2cm): Narrow part from uterus
3. Ampulla (5cm): Wider portion, FERTILIZATION site
4. Infundibulum (2cm): Funnel-shaped, fimbriae (ovarian fimbria attaches to ovary)

Functions: 1) Receives sperm, 2) Fertilization site, 3) Ovum pickup, 4) Embryo transport & nourishment

3. Uterus

Shape: Hollow, muscular, pear-shaped

Size (Non-pregnant): 7.5cm long √ó 5cm wide √ó 2.5cm depth, Wall 1.25cm thick

Location: True pelvis, between bladder & rectum

Function: Shelter fetus during pregnancy, prepares monthly, expels contents after pregnancy

Parts of Uterus (7)

B-F-C-C-I-C-O
1. Body/Corpus: Upper 2/3
2. Fundus: Domed upper wall between tube insertions
3. Cornua: Upper outer angles where tubes join
4. Cavity: Potential space, triangular (base uppermost)
5. Isthmus: 7mm narrow area, enlarges to form lower uterine segment in pregnancy
6. Cervix (Neck): Lower 1/3, 2.5cm each direction - Vaginal portion + Supravaginal portion
7. Os: Internal os (isthmus‚Üícervix), External os (end of cervix, round‚Üítransverse slit after childbirth)

Layers of Uterus (3)

P-M-E (Outside‚ÜíInside)
1. Perimetrium: Outer serous coat, peritoneal extension
2. Myometrium: Muscle coat (main bulk), smooth muscle + vessels + nerves, thick in upper uterus
3. Endometrium: Mucous membrane, columnar epithelium, cyclical changes (1-5mm thick)

Uterine Ligaments (6)

P-T-U-B-R-O
1. Pubocervical: Cervix ‚Üí Pubic bones (under bladder)
2. Transverse Cervical (Cardinal): Cervix ‚Üí Pelvic sidewalls (MOST IMPORTANT)
3. Uterosacral: Cervix ‚Üí Sacrum (vertical, support cervix)
4. Broad: Peritoneal fold over tubes (NOT true ligament, NO support)
5. Round: Fibromuscular, upper outer angles ‚Üí inguinal canal ‚Üí labia majora
6. Ovarian: Cornua ‚Üí Ovaries (between broad ligament folds)

4. Vagina

Structure: Musculo-membranous canal, in front of rectum, behind bladder

Length: Posterior wall 10cm, Anterior wall 7.5cm (cervix projects at right angle)

Shape: H-shaped in section (nulliparous), Transverse rugae (stretch during intercourse/childbirth)

Functions:

1. Allows menstruation escape, excretory duct for uterine secretions
2. Receives semen during intercourse
3. Exit for fetus during delivery

Environment: Acidic pH 4.5 (glycogen‚Üílactic acid by Doderlein's lactobacillus) - Protects against infection

Layers: Squamous epithelium + Vascular connective tissue
