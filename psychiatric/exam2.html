üî¥ Maternal Emergencies

1. Ruptured Uterus

Types: Complete (all layers) / Incomplete (endometrium + myometrium)

Causes: Over-distension, Hypertonic contraction, Obstructed labor, Grandmultipara, Trauma

S&S: Internal/external bleeding, Abdominal pain, Tachycardia, Infant easily felt

Management: Anti-shock (O2, IV, drugs, antibiotics) ‚Üí Surgery (hysterectomy or suture)

2. Shock

Types: Hypovolemic (hemorrhage) / Septic (infection) / Neurogenic (pain/trauma)

Management: O2 mask, IV fluids, Turn to side, Keep warm, Elevate legs, Antibiotics/sedatives, Monitor VS

3. PROM (Premature Rupture of Membranes)

Definition: ROM ‚â•1 hour before labor onset

üî¥ Fetal Emergencies

1. Cord Prolapse/Presentation

Prolapse: Cord descends into vagina after ROM

Presentation: Cord in front before ROM

Occult: Cord beside presenting part

Management: Knee-chest/Trendelenburg position, Manual pressure on presenting part, Warm saline gauze on cord, Monitor FHR, Prepare CS

2. Fetal Distress

Signs: Meconium (cephalic), Bradycardia (<120), Tachycardia (>160), Excessive caput/moulding

Management: Stop oxytocin, Lateral position, O2 to mother, IV fluids, Check FHR, PV exam, Prepare delivery, Episiotomy
