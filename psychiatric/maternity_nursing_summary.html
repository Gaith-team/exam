üë∂ Normal Labor & Birth

Normal Labor (Eutocia): Expulsion of mature viable fetus, vertex presentation, through birth canal, spontaneously within 24 hours, without complications

Key Terminology

  • Lie: Relationship of fetal long axis to maternal long axis (99.5% longitudinal)
  • Presentation: First fetal part entering pelvis (96% cephalic, 3.5% breech, 0.5% shoulder)
  • Position: Relationship of presenting part to maternal pelvis (e.g., ROA, LOA)
  • Attitude: Relationship of fetal parts to each other (normally full flexion)
  • Station: Descent of presenting part relative to ischial spines (-2 to +2)
  • Engagement: Biparietal diameter passes through pelvic brim
  • Effacement: Thinning/shortening of cervix
  • Crowning: Biparietal diameter distends vulva (doesn't recede)

Signs of Labor

Premonitory Signs

  • Lightening (fetal head descends - PG at 38 weeks, MG at labor onset)
  • Pressure below
  • Frequency of urination
  • False labor pains

Second Stage (Expulsion)

From full dilatation to delivery of baby
  • Duration: PG: 45-60 min, MP: 15-30 min
  • Signs: Urge to bear down, presenting part visible, grunting, rectal pressure

Mechanism of Labor (Cardinal Movements)

  1. Descent: Continuous throughout labor
  2. Engagement: Biparietal diameter enters pelvis
  3. Flexion: Chin to chest
  4. Internal Rotation: Occiput rotates anteriorly
  5. Extension: Head extends under symphysis
  6. Restitution: Head rotates to natural position
  7. External Rotation: Shoulders rotate
  8. Expulsion: Delivery of body

Third Stage (Placental)

From delivery of baby to delivery of placenta
  • Duration: 10-20 minutes (both PG & MP)

Signs of Placental Separation

  • Uterus becomes firm, globular, rises
  • Suprapubic bulge appears
  • Gush of blood
  • Umbilical cord lengthens
  • Loss of cord pulsation
