2. Hydatidiform Mole

Definition: Gross malformation of trophoblast, chorionic villi proliferate & become avascular

Risk: Age >40 or <19, Malnutrition

S&S: Excessive vomiting, Uterus larger than expected, Bleeding + vesicles, No fetal parts/movements, Positive pregnancy test 1:500 dilution

Management: Evacuation, Monitor HCG for 2 years monthly, Contraception for 2 years, Chemotherapy if HCG >5 IU/L at 8wks postpartum

3. Ectopic Pregnancy (99% tubal)

Risk: PID, Previous pelvic surgery, IUD use

S&S: Short amenorrhea, Sudden severe colicky pain (one iliac fossa), Blood-stained discharge, Shock, Dyspareunia

Management: Surgical (Salpingectomy) or Medical (Methotrexate if B-HCG <10,000)

ü©∏ Late Bleeding (‚â•28 weeks)

1. Placenta Previa

Definition: Abnormal placenta in lower uterine segment

S&S: Bright red, Painless, Recurrent bleeding; Soft pain-free uterus; Easy feel fetus/hear FHR

Degrees: Complete (covers os) / Incomplete (partial) / Lateralis (on LUS) / Marginalis (reaches os)

Management: Bed rest, NO vaginal/rectal exam, Avoid constipation/enemas, Monitor bleeding/shock, FHR q4h, IV fluids/O2

2. Abruptio Placenta

Definition: Premature separation of normally situated placenta

S&S: Abdominal pain, Dark red bleeding, Tender uterus, Hard to feel fetal parts, No FHR

Types: Revealed / Concealed / Combined

Management: Monitor VS/FHR continuously, IV transfusion, Anti-shock, Prepare for delivery/CS
